Types of Vessels
A. Iron Removal Vessel: Removes excess iron and manganese from water through specialized filtration media that oxidizes and traps these impurities. High iron levels cause staining, unpleasant taste, and damage to pipelines and appliances.
•	Prevents reddish stains on tiles, fittings, and clothes
•	Improves taste and clarity of water
•	Protects plumbing and water equipment from corrosion
B. Multi-Media Vessel: A multi-layered filtration system using graded media such as gravel, sand, and anthracite to remove turbidity, suspended solids, and organic matter. It ensures primary purification before water reaches other treatment stages.
•	Produces clear, sediment-free water
•	Enhances performance of downstream filters and purifiers
•	Increases system lifespan and efficiency
C. Carbon Vessel: Uses activated carbon media to adsorb chlorine, bad odor, color, and organic impurities, which can otherwise affect taste and harm RO membranes or UV systems.
•	Improves taste and smell of water
•	Removes harmful chlorine and organic chemicals
•	Provides safer and more pleasant water for daily use
D. Softener Vessel: Uses ion-exchange resin to remove hardness-causing minerals such as calcium and magnesium. Hard water leads to scaling, reduces soap lathering, and shortens the life of household appliances.
•	Provides soft, smooth water for bathing and cleaning
•	Extends the lifespan of washing machines, heaters, and pipes
•	Reduces detergent use and improves cleaning efficiency
